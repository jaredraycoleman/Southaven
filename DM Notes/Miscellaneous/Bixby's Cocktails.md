## Cocktail Effects

All effects last for 24 hours unless otherwise stated.
### 1. Sorrowfruit Pulp
- **Found In:** Cemeteries where deep grief lingers, often near the graves of tragic figures.
- **DM Note:** This can appear after burying someone important in the story.
- **Description:** A pulpy, deep-blue fruit that leaks a misty, tear-scented vapor.  
- **Taste:** A thick, syrupy drink with the salty-sweet taste of caramel and regret.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You are overcome with despair, suffering disadvantage on all charisma checks.
  2. **(Bad)** You weep uncontrollably, leaving a trail of tears wherever you go.
  3. **(Good)** You gain darkvision for 60 feet.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You can communicate telepathically with any creature within 30 feet.  

### 2. Pyrelily Petals
- **Found In:** The edges of active volcanoes or within the scorched footprints of fire elementals.  
- **DM Note:** Found near the dwarven forges in the Ember Halls.
- **Description:** Bright orange petals that occasionally smolder without burning.  
- **Taste:** A fiery-orange cocktail that tastes like spiced honey with a faint burning aftertaste.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your breath catches fire, dealing 1d6 damage to yourself each time you speak.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your body temperature rises uncomfortably, causing you to sweat profusely.  
  3. **(Good)** You gain resistance to fire damage.
  4. **(Very Good)** Your melee attacks deal an additional 1d6 fire damage.  

### 3. Echoing Shard
- **Found In:** Deep within caverns where the echoes never fade, often guarded by creatures that thrive on sound.  
- **DM Note**: Found in back of Southaven mines
- **Description:** A jagged crystal that hums with strange whispers when held.  
- **Taste:** A sharp, effervescent drink with the taste of citrus and static.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your voice echoes uncontrollably, making stealth impossible.  
  2. **(Bad)** You involuntarily repeat the last thing you said every minute.  
  3. **(Good)** Your words gain weight, granting you advantage on Persuasion and Deception checks.
  4. **(Very Good)** You can cast [Dissonant Whispers](https://www.dndbeyond.com/spells/2619104-dissonant-whispers?srsltid=AfmBOopWK7lKnRf9y7sFSMugMtOaxwTL__jU2LE0VfCaLPJHAQO8zIkC) once.
### 4. Moonshadow Mushroom
- **Found In:** Caves bathed in rare moonlight reflections, often appearing after lunar eclipses.  
- **DM Note:** Ancient City Dragonspire mountain
- **Description:** A bioluminescent fungus that glows under moonlight.  
- **Taste:** A smooth, creamy drink with hints of vanilla and an odd metallic aftertaste.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You have disadvantage on initiative rolls.
  2. **(Bad)** You yawn uncontrollably, making concentration nearly impossible.  
  3. **(Good)** You become invisible in dim light or darkness.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You can teleport between shadows at will.
### 5. Whispering Kelp
- **Found In:** Sunken places where ancient secrets were lost to the tides.  
- **DM Notes:** Ancient City Dragonspire mountains
- **Description:** A tangled seaweed that murmurs faint, incomprehensible phrases when touched.  
- **Taste:** A briny, slightly fizzy drink that tastes like seawater and fresh mint.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You constantly hear whispering voices, making focus impossible. Disadvantage on perception rolls.
  2. **(Bad)** Your speech becomes a garbled mess of bubbles.  Disadvantage on spells with a verbal component.
  3. **(Good)** You can breathe underwater.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You gain a swim speed of 40 feet and echolocation (can see general shapes through barriers).
### 6. Phoenixberry
- **Found In:** A phoenix's nesting ground—if the phoenix does not burn the bush first.  
- **DM Note:** a trapped Phoenix in Duskwatch keep can be rescued, and will reincarnate as soon as it is outside and feels safe. Phoenixberries will grow in the same spot the next day.
- **Description:** A bright red berry that smokes slightly when plucked.  
- **Taste:** A tangy, cinnamon-spiced drink with a warm, lingering heat.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You burst into flames, taking 2d6 fire damage.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your hair and eyebrows are singed off.  
  3. **(Good)** If reduced to 0 HP, regain 1 HP instantly.
  4. **(Very Good)** If reduced to 0 HP, regain 10 HP instantly.  
### 7. Bloodsuckle Nectar
- **Found In:** Harvested from vampiric vines growing near ancient battlefields.  
- **DM Note:** vines on duskwatch
- **Description:** A deep crimson nectar with an iron-rich scent.  
- **Taste:** A thick, syrupy drink that tastes like cherries and copper.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You lose 1d6 HP every two hours.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your veins glow visibly red.  
  3. **(Good)** You gain 10 temporary HP.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You regenerate 1 HP per minute.
### 8. Starlit Fern Frond
- **Found In:** Only sprouts under meteor showers in untouched glades.  
- **DM Note:** randomly mention a meteor shower at some point and see if characters remember to look.
- **Description:** A plant that only grows under meteor showers.  
- **Taste:** A sparkling, almost weightless drink that tastes like lavender and ozone.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You float 10 feet in the air, then fall, taking 1d6 falling damage.
  2. **(Bad)** Your skin glows brightly, making stealth impossible.  
  3. **(Good)** You can hover 5 feet off the ground at will.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You can fly at a speed of 30 feet.  
### 9. Chittering Cricket Leg
- **Found In:** A species of magical cricket found only in Feywild meadows and swamps.
- **DM Notes:** In Blacktide swamp
- **Description:** A tiny, twitching cricket leg that never stops moving.  
- **Taste:** A jittery, electric-blue drink that tastes like blueberries and static.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your leg twitches uncontrollably, giving you disadvantage on dexterity checks.
  2. **(Bad)** You develop an uncontrollable eye twitch.  
  3. **(Good)** Your movement speed increases by 10 feet.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You can jump three times your normal distance.  
### 10. Voidcap Dust
- **Found In:** Fungi that grow in absolute darkness, often in deep caves connected to the void. 
- **DM Note**: In the underground under Lusteris.
- **Description:** A fine, black powder that absorbs light unnaturally.  
- **Taste:** A pitch-black drink that tastes like nothing… and then everything all at once.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You are silenced, unable to speak or make any sound.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your body flickers between reality and void, giving you disadvantage on physical interactions (Strength & Dexterity-based checks).
  3. **(Good)** You can step into shadows and emerge up to 30 feet away as a bonus action (like a weaker Shadow Step).
  4. **(Very Good)** You exist partially in the void, gaining resistance to non-magical damage and the ability to phase through solid objects as difficult terrain.
### 11. Blinking Toadstool
- **Found In:** Shifting, enchanted forests where the landscape subtly rearranges itself at night.  
- **DM Notes:** Somewhere in the Elfwood or Witchwood.
- **Description:** A mushroom that opens and closes like an eye when touched.  
- **Taste:** A velvety, earthy drink with an odd, flickering aftertaste that seems to vanish between sips.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** During combat, you may blink randomly out of existence. On your turn, perform a DC 10 Wisdom check. On a fail, you blink out of existence for 1d4 rounds.
  2. **(Bad)** Your vision stutters, causing disadvantage on Perception checks.  
  3. **(Good)** You can cast *Blur* once per short rest.
  4. **(Very Good)** You can teleport up to 60 feet once per short rest.  
### 12. Thunderpepper Seeds
- **Found In:** Hidden within storm-wracked soil, where they are struck by lightning before becoming viable.
- **DM Notes:** Storm in Dragonspire Mountains or Pale Mountains.
- **Description:** Tiny seeds that spark when exposed to air.  
- **Taste:** A zesty, lemon-lime drink with an electrifying tang that makes your tongue tingle.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You are struck by a tiny bolt of lightning, taking 2d6 lightning damage.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your hair stands on end and emits tiny static shocks to those who touch you.  
  3. **(Good)** Your movement speed increases by 10 feet.  
  4. **(Very Good)** Your melee attacks deal an extra 1d6 lightning damage.  
### 13. Glass-Eye Beetle
- **Found In:** Abandoned wizard towers, feeding on stray magical residue.  
- **DM Notes:** Somewhere in Lusteris, maybe high council tower.
- **Description:** A translucent beetle whose shell reflects strange visions.  
- **Taste:** A swirling, iridescent drink that tastes like strawberries and copper, shifting flavors mid-sip.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You hallucinate wildly, unable to distinguish reality for 1 hour. Disadvantage on intelligence checks.
  2. **(Bad)** Your reflection moves independently.  
  3. **(Good)** You can see through illusions.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You gain *True Sight* for 10 minutes.  
### 14. Molten Marrow
- **Found In:** Extracted from the bones of creatures born in lava flows, such as magma drakes or ember giants.  
- **DM Note:** Volcanic forges in the Ember Halls or Ancient City in Dragonspire Mountains.
- **Description:** A thick, glowing orange liquid extracted from volcanic creatures.  
- **Taste:** A smoldering drink with a smoky, charred caramel flavor that leaves a pleasant warmth.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You sweat molten rock, taking 2d6 fire damage.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your body radiates heat, making you uncomfortable and steaming in the cold.  
  3. **(Good)** You gain resistance to fire damage.
  4. **(Very Good)** Your attacks ignite anything flammable on contact.  
### 15. Timewarped Ivy
- **Found In:** Ruins of ancient, time-lost civilizations, growing in places where time is warped by magic.
- **DM Notes:** somewhere in Solara/Witchwood, having to do with the plague.
- **Description:** A metallic plant that rattles when disturbed.  
- **Taste:** A smooth, slightly bitter drink with notes of licorice and steel.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** During combat, you are frozen in time for 1d4 rounds on a failed DC 10 Wisdom check.
  2. **(Bad)** You move at half speed, as if slightly out of sync.  
  3. **(Good)** You can take one extra reaction per turn.  
  4. **(Very Good)** Once per short rest, you can rewind your undo and redo your last action (6 seconds).
### 16. Chilled Wisp Essence
- **Found In:** Captured from the misty trails left behind by dying will-o'-wisps.  
- **DM Notes:** Witchwood
- **Description:** A swirling vapor that seems to flinch from touch.  
- **Taste:** A translucent, icy drink that tastes like peppermint and distant echoes.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your body becomes ghostly, unable to physically interact with objects.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your voice is barely audible, as if spoken from far away.  
  3. **(Good)** You can move through solid objects as difficult terrain.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You gain etherealness for 1 minute.  
### 17. Evermourn Bloom
- **Found In:** Grows atop the graves of those who died with unresolved regrets.
- **DM Notes:** Solara, related to a side quest.
- **Description:** A dark violet flower that wilts but never truly dies.  
- **Taste:** A bittersweet drink with notes of blackberry and deep sorrow.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You see visions of your own death, becoming frightened by any creature that mentions death.
  2. **(Bad)** You involuntarily hum a mournful tune that others find unnerving.  
  3. **(Good)** You can communicate with spirits.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You gain resistance to necrotic damage and advantage on death saving throws.  
### 18. Starfire Cactus Juice
- **Found In:** The heart of great deserts after rare celestial events like planetary alignments.  
- **DM Notes:** edge of pale mountains/dead lands, maybe when dealing with goblin camps.
- **Description:** A luminous, golden liquid harvested from a rare desert plant.  
- **Taste:** A glowing, golden drink that fizzes pleasantly, tasting of citrus and sunlight.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** You radiate blinding light, making you unable to hide.  
  2. **(Bad)** Your skin becomes hot to the touch, causing mild discomfort.  
  3. **(Good)** You can cast *Daylight* once.  
  4. **(Very Good)** Your attacks deal an additional 1d6 radiant damage.  
### 19. Ironroot Bark
- **Found In:** Ancient, enchanted trees in fae realms, rumored to be older than time itself.  
- **DM Notes:** somewhere in Dragonspire
- **Description:** A gnarled piece of bark as hard as steel but flexible when chewed.  
- **Taste:** A thick, woody drink that tastes like aged whiskey and resilience.  
- **Effects (Roll 1d4):**  
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your muscles stiffen, giving you disadvantage on Dexterity-based checks.  
  2. **(Bad)** You speak in slow, drawn-out syllables.  
  3. **(Good)** Your Strength score increases by 2.  
  4. **(Very Good)** You gain resistance to non-magical physical damage.
### 20. Tidalglass Coral
- **Found In**: Coastal reefs that have been exposed to powerful storms.
- **DM Notes**: Found at Seawatch, often after storms or shipwrecks.
- **Description**: A translucent blue coral that shimmers like liquid glass.
- **Taste**: A crisp, refreshing drink that tastes like sea breeze and chilled citrus.
- **Effects (Roll 1d4)**:
  1. **(Very Bad)** Your skin turns slick and translucent, making gripping objects difficult (disadvantage on strength-based checks).
  2. **(Bad)** Your voice sounds warped, like it's being spoken through water.
  3. **(Good)** You can walk on water.
  4. **(Very Good)** You can control a 30-foot cube of water, shaping it at will.
