**Original Description:**

Description

King Aldric IX Solborn is a man in his early sixties, his once-golden hair now silvered with age and stress. He carries himself with practiced regal bearing—shoulders back, chin high—though those close to him notice the weight of his crown growing heavier with each passing year. His blue eyes, the traditional mark of the Solborn line, have lost some of their youthful fire, replaced with a calculating weariness.

He dresses in the traditional white and gold of Solaran royalty, with Elandria's sunburst embroidered prominently on his breast. His crown is elegant but not ostentatious—a circlet of gold inlaid with amber and citrine that catches the light beautifully. He wears the Ring of Solborn, a signet ring passed down through every monarch since King Aldric I, bearing the royal seal.

Unlike the warrior-kings of old, Aldric IX is a politician and administrator first. His hands are soft, his frame lean rather than muscular, and he moves with the careful deliberation of a man who knows every gesture is observed and judged.

Personality

King Aldric IX is a pragmatist trapped between tradition and necessity. He understands that Solara's strength lies in its partnership with The Church of Elandria, yet he quietly resents how thoroughly the Church has entwined itself with royal authority. He cannot make major decisions without the Luminarch's approval, cannot pass laws the Church opposes, and cannot even choose his own advisors without clerical input.

Despite this frustration, he is neither foolish nor rebellious. He maintains the appearance of perfect devotion to Elandria, attending services, hosting Church ceremonies, and publicly deferring to the Luminarch's wisdom. Behind closed doors, however, he carefully maneuvers to preserve what royal autonomy remains.

Aldric is intelligent and politically astute. He thinks several moves ahead, considers consequences carefully, and avoids rash action. Some see this as wisdom; others see it as indecisiveness. He prefers negotiation to warfare, compromise to confrontation, and stability to radical change.

He can be cold when necessary, sacrificing individuals for the greater good of the realm, but he takes no pleasure in such decisions. He drinks wine to help him sleep, and those who serve him late at night sometimes hear him pacing his chambers, wrestling with choices that have no good outcomes.

Background

Early Life (Born 1205)

Aldric was born the third son of King Marcus IV Solborn, never expecting to inherit the throne. His eldest brother, Crown Prince Darian, was being groomed for kingship, while his second brother, Prince Elric, pursued military command. Young Aldric was educated at the Grand Cathedral, where he studied theology, history, and diplomacy. The Church hoped to groom him for a high clerical position—perhaps even Luminarch one day.

This education proved invaluable when tragedy struck. In 1235, a plague swept through Solaris. Crown Prince Darian died, followed weeks later by Prince Elric. At age thirty, Aldric suddenly became heir to the throne.

Heir and Preparation (1235-1251)

The unexpected change devastated Aldric, who had loved his brothers deeply. The Church immediately shifted its attention to him, ensuring he would be a pious and cooperative monarch. The Luminarch at the time, Theodric Brightspire (who still serves today), took personal responsibility for "refining" Aldric's education.

Aldric married Lady Elendra Ashmore in 1238, a match arranged by his father and the Church. The marriage was cordial but not passionate. Elendra was devout, beautiful, and politically connected—everything a future queen should be. She gave him two children: Princess Mariella (born 1239) and Prince Aldric (born 1241).

When King Marcus IV died in 1251, Aldric IX was crowned in an elaborate ceremony at the Grand Cathedral. He was 46 years old and had spent sixteen years preparing for the role he never wanted.

Reign (1251-Present)

Aldric's sixteen-year reign has been marked by stability, prosperity, and caution:

The Early Years (1251-1260):
Focused on consolidating power and establishing good relations with major lords
Successfully negotiated improved trade relations with Lusteris, despite Church opposition
Avoided military conflicts, preferring diplomacy
Managed succession of several major lordships peacefully

The Tragedy (1257):
Prince Aldric, his son and heir, died at age 16 from a riding accident
Queen Elendra, grief-stricken, withdrew from public life
Princess Mariella, now the heir, was already married to a minor noble and living far from court
The succession question became precarious

Recent Years (1260-1267):
Growing reports of cult activity across the kingdom
The Order of the Black Star has become increasingly bold
Relations with the Church have grown more strained as the Luminarch pushes for more aggressive action against heresy
Queen Elendra died in 1264, leaving Aldric isolated
He has not remarried, despite Church pressure to produce more heirs
The Siege of Southaven (1267, Seventh Month) represents the greatest crisis of his reign

Relationships

Luminarch Theodric Brightspire

The High Priest of Elandria has been Aldric's advisor, mentor, and jailer for over thirty years. Theodric groomed Aldric for kingship and expects absolute cooperation. Their relationship is complex—genuine respect mixed with frustration and resentment. Theodric sees himself as Aldric's spiritual father; Aldric sees him as an obstacle to true royal authority.

Princess Mariella Ashmore (Daughter)

Aldric's only surviving child. She married Lord Brennan Ashmore of [[Sunset Bluff]] and lives far from court. The marriage was arranged before her brother's death, and Aldric now regrets sending her away. She visits Solaris occasionally, but their relationship is distant. She has given him two grandchildren, though neither is in direct line of succession.

Lord Elrin Kael

Lord of Southaven and one of Aldric's more reliable vassals. Aldric respects Elrin's military competence and has granted him significant autonomy in governing the northern territories. When the Siege of Southaven began, Aldric dispatched aid quickly—both because Southaven is strategically vital and because he genuinely trusts Elrin's leadership.

Lord Cathan Morwen (Lord of Seawatch)

Aldric's most powerful vassal and the commander of the Solaran navy. Cathan is ambitious and politically savvy, constantly maneuvering for greater influence at court. Aldric keeps him satisfied with honors and responsibilities while watching him carefully.

Master Thandril Greymoor (Master of Coin)

A non-noble commoner elevated to the Royal Council for his financial brilliance. Aldric trusts him implicitly and relies on his counsel for economic matters. The Church disapproves of a commoner holding such high office, but Thandril's results are undeniable.

Current Situation (Year 1267, Seventh Month)

King Aldric faces multiple crises simultaneously:

The Siege of Southaven

The Order of the Black Star has launched a coordinated assault on one of his most important lordships. He has dispatched Landor Vess|General Landor Vess with elven troops (a politically risky move that required Church approval), but he fears it may not be enough. If Southaven falls, the entire northern frontier becomes vulnerable.

The Succession Crisis

At 62, Aldric is not old for a king, but he is not young either. He has no male heir, and his daughter's children are not in direct line of succession. The Church is pressuring him to remarry and produce more heirs, but Aldric has resisted. Some whisper he is secretly grooming Princess Mariella to rule after him, which would be controversial—Solara has never had a reigning queen.

Church-Crown Tensions

Luminarch Theodric has become increasingly aggressive in pushing Church authority. He wants the Crown to declare martial law and give Church inquisitors sweeping powers to root out cult influence. Aldric fears this would turn Solara into a theocracy in all but name. The standoff has paralyzed decision-making at the highest levels.

The Lusteris Question

With cult activity escalating, some advisors suggest seeking military alliance with Lusteris, despite the political scandal this would cause. Lusteris has proven adept at fighting in harsh conditions, and their freedom from Church influence might make them valuable allies against the Black Star. Aldric is quietly exploring this option through back channels.

Personal Isolation

Since Queen Elendra's death, Aldric has become increasingly isolated. He trusts few people, sleeps poorly, and drinks more than he should. Some courtiers whisper that the king is losing his edge, becoming paranoid and indecisive. Others see a man carrying impossible burdens with quiet dignity.
